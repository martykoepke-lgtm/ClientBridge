01 / Logos

Logo Mark Concepts

Five directions ranging from abstract to literal. Each works at favicon size (16px), nav bar size (32px), and marketing size (120px+).

Concept A
ClientBridge
The Bridge
Literal bridge silhouette. Two pillars represent client and developer; the span is the connection. Clean geometric lines. Works as a favicon by simplifying to just the arch.
Concept B
ClientBridge
The Bracket
Code brackets { } with a connecting bridge line between two nodes. Speaks to developers instantly while remaining abstract enough for clients. The dots represent the two users being connected.
Concept C
ClientBridge
The Relay
Two screens (client app and developer dashboard) with data flowing between them. The center node is the bridge — the point where feedback, context, and progress converge. Communicates the product function directly.
Concept D
clientbridge
The Arrow
Bold directional arrow — feedback flows one way, progress flows back. The merged arrow-and-bar shape is confident and minimal. Works beautifully as a solid favicon. Strongest at small sizes.
Concept E — Recommended
ClientBridge
ClientBridge
The Monogram
Lowercase "cb" in a rounded square. This is the Linear/Vercel approach — dead simple, instantly recognizable, works at every size from browser tab to billboard. The rounded square becomes the app icon on every platform. No metaphor to decode, no cleverness that ages poorly. Just a mark that becomes synonymous with the product through usage.

This is the direction I'd push toward. It's the most scalable, the most modern, and the hardest to get wrong in implementation.

05 / Summary

Recommended Stack

01

Logo: The Monogram

Lowercase "cb" in a rounded square. Scales from favicon to invoice header. No metaphor to explain — it just becomes the mark through usage. Add the wordmark "ClientBridge" next to it for full lockup.

02

Type: Inter Everything

One typeface, multiple weights. Inter 800 for display, 600 for headings, 400 for body. JetBrains Mono for technical elements. No font loading complexity, no pairing to get wrong.

03

Color: Warm Signal

Monochrome base (#09090B through #FAFAFA) with #F59E0B as the single accent. Amber is warm, premium, and distinctive — it stands out from every other dev tool using green or blue. It says "attention here" without shouting.

Pick the directions that resonate and I can build it out — update the codebase, generate actual logo files (SVG + PNG at all sizes), create a favicon, and apply the brand across the dashboard, review interface, invoice template, and landing page.
